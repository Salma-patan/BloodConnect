Why is blood so important?

Healthy adults have 5 to 6 litres of blood circulating throughout their body. Each drop of blood contains red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma. Blood carries oxygen and nutrients to cells while moving waste to the liver and kidneys. This ongoing process keeps us alive.

Who needs blood donation?

Patients may need blood for:

  • Heavy bleeding
  • Surgery
  • Trauma
  • Organ transplant
  • Cancer treatment
  • Blood diseases
  • Gastrointestinal diseases

Why is donating blood important?

Donating blood is important because blood has a short life and there is constant need. Platelets can only be stored for 5 to 7 days, while red blood cells can be stored for up to 42 days.
